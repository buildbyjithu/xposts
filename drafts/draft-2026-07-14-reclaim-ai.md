Software Architect.

Father of two young kids.

Between stand-ups, architecture reviews, and context switching, I lost about 3 hours every day to calendar chaos. I had maybe 90 minutes of real focused work.

This week I found a breakthrough.

I tried Calendly, Microsoft Bookings, manual time-blocking. They were useful but limited. I still faced privacy concerns:
Where is my scheduling data going?
What patterns are being inferred?
Who can see my calendar?

That led me down the AI-first scheduling path.

I discovered Reclaim AI.

It automatically prioritizes my tasks, balances meetings, and protects my focus time.

Now I can protect the hours that matter before someone else claims them.

Less firefighting.
Less reactive scheduling.
More deep work.
More family dinners on time.
More time with the people who matter most.

One thing AI keeps teaching me is that small changes can lead to big gains.
